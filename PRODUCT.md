# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Academic and agricultural-science audiences: USDA-NIFA program officers reviewing the funded project, animal-science and computer-vision researchers, prospective graduate students, and collaborators. They arrive to understand what the SARA·Detect project is, read its results, and find the papers, team, and contacts. Context: desktop and mobile, often from an emailed link or a final-report reference.

## Product Purpose
A public research-showcase site for "Detecting Subacute Ruminal Acidosis with a Real-Time Deep Learning Algorithm" (USDA-NIFA grant 2023-70001-40997, SIU Carbondale). It explains the science (optical gas imaging + lightweight segmentation), presents benchmark results, lists the three papers (CarboFormer, FUME, VLMDual), introduces the team, and records competitions/outreach. Success = a credible, legible, citable home that a reviewer or researcher trusts at a glance.

## Brand Personality
Scientific, precise, institutional-but-warm. Three words: rigorous, grounded, confident. It should read like a well-set journal/lab page, not a SaaS landing page. Evidence over hype.

## Anti-references
SaaS marketing gloss (gradient-hero gimmicks, hero-metric template, glassmorphism), startup buzzword copy, stocky generic AI-landing-page look. No playful or consumer styling that undercuts scientific credibility.

## Design Principles
- Evidence first: real numbers, real photos, real citations carry the page.
- Institutional restraint: oxblood + cream + ink, one serif/one sans/one mono, generous structure.
- Legibility is non-negotiable: strong contrast, comfortable measure, clear hierarchy at every breakpoint.
- Quiet craft: smooth states and motion that reassure, never decorate.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA. Body/label text ≥4.5:1; visible keyboard focus on every interactive element; respect prefers-reduced-motion; touch targets ≥44px on mobile; meaningful alt text on all imagery.
